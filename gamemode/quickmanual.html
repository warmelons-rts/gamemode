How to join a team

You have likely been automatically assigned a team. If not you may type "/join #". You can also type /join "color" to join a team.

Server administrators may only allow so many players per team. Pick another team to join.
Any time a player joins a team it automatically locks so that they can keep out unwanted teammates. The team leader can unlock the team by typing "/lock" or invite people trough the f4 menu.

How to play

WMRTS got several tools that you use, they are all located in a sub-section called warmelons. You can however also use other tools that the server have, which makes building contraptions possible.

To spawn melons or props you need a spawnpoint. It is a very important structure for your team, as if only 1 team has one, they will win.
The first spawnpoint is free, but after that they cost nrg. So if you want a forward base the outpost might be a better choice. You can not spawn melons normally at outposts, but you can spawn props and barracks.

To earn nrg you have to send melons to capture points. The more capture points you have, the higher income and max melon count you have.
On some maps there is resource nodes. You can build harvesters which travel between those nodes and your spawnpoint (or outpost).

You can also find communication uplinks on some maps. If you capture them a tool named melon strike is enabled. When you use it a carnister lands on the designated area with a minor explosion, and then release 6 small fighters.

Melons take damage if in water, with the exceptions of harvesters and marine melons.

For more information check out the encyclopedia (F2).

Key binds

To order your melons, you can either use the swep in slot 3, with left mouse button to select and right mouse button to order, or you can bind keys to +wmselect and wmorder.
Key Selecting Ordering
SPRINT You add melons to the selection. you give them waypoints to follow.
DUCK Select all of same type and within 1500 radius as melon you aim at. Combine with use to lower it to 500, but also select uncommandable melons. The melon patrols between its current position and its ordered positions.
WALK Nothing, unless combined with duck. Then radius is unlimited. Forces the melon to target anything. Finds a new target if unable to hit it.
USE Selects only the melon you aim at. Melon will follow the target after executing all other waypoints.
Using the WMSTanceselect key or the swep's reload, you can set melons stances.
Key Stance
Hold Fire(USE) These melons will not fire under any circumstances. Barracks set to hold fire will not produce melons.
Fire at Will(JUMP) This is the standard setting for all melons.
Stand Ground Standard oblivious melon behavior everyone knows and loves.
Defensive(SPRINT) Will autochase any unit in the area around the melon. If the target runs away the melon will move straight back and resume what it was doing.
Offensive(WALK) Similar to defensive, but the melon will give up after chasing far beyond what a defencive melon would chase.
Berserk(DUCK) These are for melons you don't care about coming home. Berserk melons will chase any enemy coming within a large radius of it and will not stop hunt it.
